Agenda Note

File No. FC-6/GJB-68/2023-GNR

 

Sub: Proposal for Diversion of 9.85 ha Protected Forest land for the project of Widening and strengthening of Valsad-Gundlav Khergam road km 2/0 to 16/0 Taluka-Valsad District Valsad in favour of Executive Engineer, R&B Division, Valsad (FP/GJ/ROAD/152124/2022)-regarding.

 

Part-A

 

  1.  The State Govt. of Gujarat vide letter No. FED/FLP/e-file/6/2023/1344/F dated 14.06.2023 recommended the proposal for diversion of 9.85 ha Protected Forest land for the project of Widening and strengthening of Valsad-Gundlav Khergam road km 2/0 to 16/0 Taluka-Valsad District Valsad in favour of Executive Engineer, R&B Division, Valsad and forwarded it to Regional Office Gandhinagar for in-principal under Section-2 of the Forest (Conservation), Act, 1980.
  2. The facts related to the proposal as contained in the State Government’s letters dated 14.06.2023 are given below in the form of fact sheet:

Part-B

1. After the examination of the proposal at Regional Office Gandhinagar it was found that the proposal involves violation of Van (Sanrakshan evam Samvardhan) Adhiniyam, 1980 and EDS was issued vide letter dated 13.07.2023 on the following points:-

 

(i)  The Deputy Conservator of Forest has informed that the violation of FCA, 1980 is still in progress. The action taken by the concerned DCF for violation of FCA, 1980 shall be submitted.

 

(ii)  As per MoEF&CC letter dated 15.05.2023 and Gazette Notification No. C.G.L.SA-03062023-246258 "Based on the recommendation of Advisory Committee and approval of the same be the competent authority of the MoEF&CC, New Delhi, the central government hereby authorized officers of the rank of Division Forest Officer (DFO)/ Deputy Conservator of Forests (DCF) and above of the State Government or Union Territory Administration concerned, having jurisdiction over the forest land in respect of which any offense under the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 is committed or violation of the provisions of the said Act has been made, to file complaints against such person/ authority/ organization, prime facia found guilty of offense  under the Act or the violation of the rules made there under, in the court having jurisdiction in the matter.

 

2.      In response to this, State Govt. vide letter dated 06.11.2023  submitted the following information:

 

3.         After examination of the proposal, the following observed by Regional Office Gandhinagar and communicated to State Government vide letter dated 05.12.2023:

(i)  The User Agency has violated the provisions of Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980. An amount of Rs.2,16,329/- was recovered from the User Agency for the violation under IFA, 1927. However, the State Government has not submitted the details of persons/officers responsible for the violation of FCA, 1980. Further, State Govt. was requested to initiate action under FCA, 1980 vide this office EDS letter dated 13.07.2023. Neither action is taken by the State Government in this regard nor submitted any clarification. Thus, State Govt. is requested to submit the satisfactory reply of EDS letter dated 13.07.2023.

(ii)  This proposal was submitted by the User Agency on PARIVESH Portal on 13.07.2022 i.e. after 29.06.2022. Thus, State Government is requested to submit this proposal on PARIVESH 2.0 in the scenario for "Regularization of Encroachment".

 

4.     Decision of REC :-The proposal was placed before 16th meeting of REC held on 18.01.2024 (Copy of MoM attached ). After thorough deliberation and discussion with Nodal Officer, Govt. of Gujarat, the Regional Empowered Committee (REC) recommended the proposal for diversion of 9.85 Ha protected forest land for the project of widening and strengthening of Valsad-Gundlav Khergam road km 2/0 to 16/0 Taluka-Valsad District Valsad in favour of Executive Engineer, R & B Division, Valsad with general, standard and following specific conditions:-

 

(i)  The user agency shall make payment for Penalty for violation of the Van (Sanrakshan Evam Samvardhan) Adhiniyam, 1980 equal to NPV of the forest land per hectare for each year of violation from the date of actual violation with maximum up to five (5) times of the NPV plus 12 percent simple interest till the deposit is made.

 

(ii)  The User Agency shall carry out Penal CA at its cost on equal DFL for carrying out the work in violation of the Van (Sanrakshan Evam Samvardhan) Adhiniyam, 1980.

 

(iii)  The Action has to be taken under Section 3A & 3B for violation of the Van (Sanrakshan Evam Samvardhan) Adhiniyam, 1980 against Shri S. S Patel, Deputy Executive Engineer, R&B Division Valsad.

 

(iv)  The user agency shall construct 6 feet high wall along the boundary of the proposed CA land at its cost.

 

(v)  The user agency shall provide at its cost required number of Solar Pumps as given by the Forest Department for irrigating the CA plantation.

 

(vi)  User Agency shall construct underpass of height 3 to 4 Meters and 5-meter width at its cost at every 500 meters within forest land along the road for easy movement of wild animals.

5.    Head of Regional Office Gandhinagar recommended and submitted the proposal for approval of Hon'ble Minister to accept the recommendation of REC and for according In-principle/Stage-I approval for the above proposal. 

6.The proposal for road construction has been recommended by the REC and submitted for the approval of  HMEFCC by the Regional Office as the same involves violation. However, it has been observed that the same proposal has also been uploaded on PARIVESH 2.0 wherein following EDS raised by the Ministry: -

  1. As per the DSS analysis, the width of the proposed Road alignment is found varying from 5 meter (LHS & RHS) to 3 meter (LHS & RHS). Therefore, the same needs clarification. 
  2. The justification for locating the project in the forest land has not been submitted by the user agency on PARIVESH. The same shall be uploaded. 
  3. The present proposal has been submitted under the Scenario-2 whereas the present the proposal involves Violations under the Van (Sanrakshan Evam Samvardhan) Adhiniyam, 1980. Therefore, the instant proposal needs submission under the Scenario-5.
  4. As per the details of violation reported in the online part-ii form of PARIVESH, it has been reported that the forest offence booked & investigation is underway. However, details like action taken against the person responsible for violation has not been submitted/ uploaded. This needs clarification along with the action taken report shall be submitted. 
  5. As per the online Part-II form the total number of trees to be felled reported as 703 whereas the Deputy Conservator of Forests, Valsad through their SIR informed that total 700 number of trees are to be felled in the present project. Therefore, correct details of total number of trees to be felled shall be updated in online Part-II form. 
  6. Two Site inspection reports of the Deputy Conservator of Forests, Valsad have been uploaded on PARIVESH from which it is not clear which one is the final and may be considered for the present project. Hence, the same needs to be examined and final copy of SIR shall be uploaded on PARIVESH. 
  7. An undertaking from the user agency to bear the cost of CA, NPV, Penal NPV etc has not been uploaded on PARIVESH which needs submission. 
  8. The user agency in the instant proposal reported that the Non-forest land is not required for the project whereas the same proposal has been uploaded in PARIVESH 1.0 with proposal No FP/GJ/ROAD/152124/2022 wherein it has been informed that total 9.8 ha Non-forest land is also required to execute the project. This needs to be examined by the State Govt. and factual position shall be updated on PARIVESH. 
  9. It has been reported that the CA site comprising 20 ha land has been identified in the degraded forest land which is situated in the Village Dharoi, Range-Rdf Khedbrahma under Sabar Kantha District against the diversion of 9.85 ha protected forest land. However, as per provisions given under sub-rule (1) rule 13 of the Van (Sanrakshan evam samvardhan) Rules, 2023, the Compensatory afforestation (CA) has to be raised over equivalent non-forest land which is not under the management and administrative control of the forest department. Therefore, CA site needs to be identified over the Non-forest land instead of forest land in the present proposal. 
  10. Plantation scheme, suitability certificate, DGPS map, Survey of India toposheet map, KML file of the CA site proposed over the Non-forest land shall be uploaded on PARIVESH. 
  11. The Survey of India (SoI) topo-sheet map showing the proposed forest land for diversion shall be uploaded on the PARIVESH. 
  12. As per the online Part-II form the Eco class-I has been mentioned against the forest land proposed for diversion. Same needs to be re-verified based on ground truthing and working plan document of the area.  

7. The point-wise reply received from State Government and uploaded on PARIVESH 2.0 on 17.2.2024 are as follows:-

 

 

 

 

Comments of FC Division:-

1. The proposal has been recommended  by REC as per discussion held in meeting dated 18.1.2024 with with general, standard and  specific conditions as mentioned above. One of the condition imposed is that the User Agency shall carry out Penal CA at its cost on equal DFL for carrying out the work in violation of the Van (Sanrakshan Evam Samvardhan) Adhiniyam, 1980. 

2. As per rule 13 (4 (f)) of Van (Sanrakshan Evam Samvardhan) Rules, 2023,   special dispensation for raising compensatory afforestation over degraded forest land, minimum double inextent, may be considered in respect of following proposals, namely.—

construction of link roads, small water works, minor irrigation works, school building, dispensaries,hospital, tiny rural industrial sheds of the Government or any other similar work excluding mining and encroachment cases, which directly benefit the people of the area in hill districts and in other districts having forest area exceeding 50% of the total geographical area, provided diversion of forest area does not exceed 5 hectares.

However, in the instant proposal CA land has been proposed on degraded forest land. 

In view of the above facts, the proposal is submitted for consideration of Advisory Committee.

Proposal No: FP/MH/OTHERS/413297/2023

 

Subject: Diversion of 2.3 ha Reserve forest land for strengthening and up-gradation of Morni to Metshindi -District Border Road MDR-120 for Morni, Arav, Valvan, Metshindi Villagers in Taluka Mahabaleshwar, District Satara of Maharashtra State  – regarding. 

Part A

  1. The Govt. of Maharashtra vide their letter No FLD-2023/C.R.85/F-10 dated 24.11.2023 forwarded a fresh proposal to obtain prior approval of the Central Government, in terms of the Section-2 of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 for diversion of 2.3 ha Reserve forest land for strengthening and up-gradation of Morni to Metshindi - District Border Road MDR-120 for Morni, Arav, Valvan, Metshindi Villagers in Taluka Mahabaleshwar, District Satara of Maharashtra State. 
  2. The above said proposal has been received in the Ministry on 24.11.2023.

Brief Chronology of the Proposal: 

  1. The extant proposal for diversion of 2.30 ha reserved forest land has been submitted by the Public Work Division (West), Satara (user agency) on dated 10.01.2023 on the PARIVESH (Proposal No. FP/MH/OTHERS/413297/2023). 
  2. The State Govt. vide its letter No FLD-2023/C.R.85/F-10 dated 21.04.2023 forwarded the proposal to the RO, Nagpur of this Ministry. However, the RO, Nagpur received the proposal through PARIVESH on date 24.04.2023. 
  3. The RO, Nagpur during the scrutiny of the proposal reported that the proposal is having violation. Therefore, as per Rule 9(5)(a) of Forest (Conservation) Rule, 2022, the proposal has been returned by the RO, Nagpur to the State Government with a request to re-submit the proposal along with report on violation under the FCA, 1980 on Parivesh 2.0 so that the proposal can directly reach the Ministry (Headquarter Level) for its further consideration. 
  4. Thereafter, the State Govt. has submitted the present diversion proposal through PARIVESH on date 24.11.2023 to the Ministry.

Brief Background of the Proposal:

  1. Chaturbet – Dabhedabhekar – Kharoshi – Rule – Pimpri – Aaklpe – Uchat – Morani - Arav - Valvan - Metshindi was Other District Roads (ODR). This road is upgraded to MDR-120 standard vide State Govt. GR No. RDP-2019/P.K.02/Planning-2 dated no 11/02/2020 as per Road Development Plan 2001-2021 of Govt. of Maharashtra. Total length of this road is 99.740 Km which starts at Chaturbet (Km.0/00) and ends near Metshindi (Km.99/740) at the Border of Satara & Ratnagiri District. Details of the Chaturbet (Km.0/00) to Metshindi (Km.99/740) Road are as follows; 

 

  1. The Deputy Director, Sahyadri Tiger Reserve, Kolhapur in its Site inspection report informed that the length from Km.0/00 to Km 85/000 of this road is Black Topped, with 3.75 m. wide carriageway & 10.00 m. average road way width. There is prime demand of peoples from Morni, Arav, Valvan & Shindi to Upgrade the existing WBM road from Morni to Met Shindi - District Border for their daily needs and medical, educational facility at Mahabaleshwar in Satara District, at Khed in Ratnagiri District. The length of this route beyond Satara District Border Near Metshindi to Bharne Naka Khed in Ratnagiri District is 5.5-meter-wide carriageway with Bituminous surface and in good condition up to Mumbai-Goa National Highway.
  2. The total forest area involved in the instant proposal is 2.30 Ha Reserved Forest land and the details of total land involved in the project are as follows; 

 

Justification For Locating Project In Forest Area:-

  1. Project will create connectivity between western Maharashtra and Konkan Region and facilitate communication and connectivity to the people of 16 villages of Kandat valley, which are remotest villages in Satara district and bring them in main flow of development. Also, in future, it will be helpful to enhance eco-tourism to the great extends.

FACT SHEET

 

 

Part- B

The DSS analysis revealed as under:

 

  1. Proposed forest area for diversion falls under Satara district of Maharashtra State.
  2. As per DSS, the proposed forest land for diversion is located inside the Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary.
  3. Forest Cover in the instant project: 1 ha of land is characterized with Moderately Dense Forest (MDF) and 1 ha is Non-Forest/ Non-wooded in terms of forest classes (as per the ISFR 2021) based on the interpretation of satellite data period 2019.
  4. Forest Types details: 5/DS1 Dry Deciduous Scrub, 5A/C3 Southern Dry Mixed Deciduous Forest: 1 ha, 8A/C2 Western Sub Tropical Hill Forest: 1 ha and Non-Forest: 1 ha
  5. The instant proposal falls under Inviolate or In high conservation zone (NHCV) category as per the DSS Rule-1 because the proposed forest land for diversion is located inside Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary. Final result is Inviolate.   
  6. The proposed CA site is located at a distance of 65.31 Km from the forest land proposed for diversion. 

Part-C

 

  1. From the examination of the said proposal certain shortfalls was observed and same has been communicated on dated 07.12.2023 and the Govt. of Maharashtra on 13.12.2023 submitted point wise reply as under:

 

 

  1. The Site Inspection was carried out by Regional Office, Nagpur on 05.01.2024. Remarks by the DDGF (Central) Regional Office, MoEF&CC, Nagpur are as under:
  2. The forest land proposed for diversion falls within the boundary of Koyna Wildlife Sanctuary which forms a part of buffer area of Sahyadri Tiger Reserve. The SC has approved the proposal subject to certain conditions. The approval may be given subject to fulfilment of all conditions stipulated by the SC NBWL.
  3. Non-Forest Land measuring 2.3 ha has been identified in the village Valvan, Taluka Mahabaleshwar, Dist. Satara (Gat No. 21) for raising compensatory afforestation. However, due to high crown density, the plantation has been proposed over degraded forest land measuring 2.3 ha in village Andora, Tal Khandala, Dist Satara (Compartment No. 801).
  4. In part-II, the DCF has mentioned that “the area is slightly susceptible to the erosion but, susceptible to land slide due to area receives heavy rain falls every year”. Since the trees to be felled are on steep slope, suitable engineering measures may be adopted to prevent landslides.
  5. The proposal was placed before the Regional Empowered Committee, RO, Nagpur in its 109th Meeting dated 16/11/2023. During the discussion, the Committee noted that:
    1. A cart track from Shindi-Metshindi to Morni is visible in old Survey of India toposheet no. 47 G 10 published in the year 1946 for Ratnagiri and Satara District which was surveyed during the years 1882-83.
    2. A road from Shindi to Morni is visible in old Survey of India incomplete toposheet 47 G 10 published in the year 1973 for Ratnagiri and Satara District which was surveyed during the year 1966-67.

The Committee took note of the existence of an old track from Metshindi to Morni from the above-mentioned toposheets corroborated by a road plan for Valvan- Akalpa- Pipri road published in the road plan booklet for the district Satara during 1961-81 as pointed out by the Nodal Officer. After detailed discussion and examination of the proposal and recommendation of State Government the Committee recommended the following:

  1. Since the proposal has been submitted in PARIVESH 2.0 and involves violation of the provisions of the FCA, 1980, it shall be sent back to the State Government for the change in scenario and its onward transmission to the MoEFCC for further consideration along with the recommendation of the REC. 

Part-D

  1. On examination of the  information submitted by the State government 13.12.2023 it was observed that the State Govt. was earlier requested to submit the KML file of the Non-forest land proposed for Compensatory Afforestation, however the same was not submitted on PARIVESH portal. Later on, the KML file of the CA area proposed over 2.3 ha non-forest land in Village- Valvan, Taluka- Mahabaleshwar, District- Satara (Gat No. 21) has been sent through email, however the same is not correct.
  2. Therefore the Ministry on dated 12.01.2024 requested the State Govt. as under:

“The State Government was requested to submit the KML file of the Non-forest land proposed for Compensatory Afforestation; however the same was not submitted on PARIVESH portal. Later on, the KML file of the CA land proposed over 2.3 ha non-forest land in Village- Valvan, Taluka- Mahabaleshwar, District- Satara (Gat No. 21) has been sent through email, however the same is not correct. Correct KML file therefore needs to be submitted”.

  1. In this reference, the State Govt. as well as Nodal Officer, Govt. of Maharashtra on dated 06.02.2024 has forwarded the letter of Deputy Director, Sahyadri Tiger Reserve, Kolhapur dated 25.01.2024 wherein a certificate has been enclosed mentioning that:

 

It is certified that the said area (Survey no. 21, in Village-Valvan, Taluka-Mahabaleshwar, District- Satara) which is non-forest land (Revenue land- Mulaki pad) is inspected by RFO and field staff along with staff of Land records department, Revenue department & User agency PWD department. And demarcation of 2.30 ha area is done with DGPS. This office checked the KML and found correct. And the status of said land is non- forest and it is verified as per land records of forest department and revenue department. The Appropriate actions will be taken to modify the errors in maps provided by State Govt. to the DSS, GoI.   

 

Part-D

Comments of FC Division:

  1. The State Government was requested to submit the KML file of the Non-forest land proposed for Compensatory Afforestation; however the same was not submitted on PARIVESH portal.
  2. Later on, the KML file of the CA land proposed over 2.3 ha non-forest land in Village- Valvan, Taluka- Mahabaleshwar, District- Satara (Gat No. 21) has been sent through email. As per the analysis of the kml file  the area proposed for CA was found to be on forest land. However, in this regard the Deputy Director, Sahyadri Tiger Reserve, Kolhapur on dated 25.01.2024 has enclosed a certificate that the status of said land is non- forest and it is verified as per land records of forest department and revenue department. The Appropriate actions will be taken to modify the errors in maps provided by State Govt. to the DSS, GoI.
